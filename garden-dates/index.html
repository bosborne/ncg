Garden Dates

The application deadline is February 27.

The General Garden Meeting is on Thursday March 31 at 7:00 PM, Nyack Senior Center, 90 Depew Avenue. Attendance is required.

The garden staking will place on Saturday April 2, 8:00 AM.

The garden will open on Sunday April 3.

Many vegetables do well in the spring, and you will get good germination because of the moister soil. You can plant transplants or sow seed for arugula, lettuce, members of the cabbage family (cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower, and kale), mache, spinach, carrots, beets, onions, or peas. There are also many kinds of Asian greens that grow quickly and like cool weather. Here's a Zone 7 planting calendar.

Your garden must be fully planted by Monday May 30.

If you have not fully planted your garden by this day then your plot will be given to someone on the waiting list. In the past some gardeners have put very little effort into their gardens but this is no longer acceptable with such a high demand for garden plots.

May 31 - June 7. Unplanted plots will be assigned to persons on waiting list.

April 3 - November 6. Planting, growing, maintaining and harvesting garden, donating extra produce to Plant A Row Project/Food Bank, participating in the Garden Contest, performing committee tasks, and having fun at the garden.

The garden contest is July 21.

Winners receive ribbons! There's also an award for the best cluster of plots.

There will be a potluck dinner on October 20 at 6:30, Nyack Senior Center, 90 Depew Avenue.

The garden will close on Sunday November 6. All items most be removed and plots restored to state they had on March 31.

The Garden cleanup is Saturday November 12.
